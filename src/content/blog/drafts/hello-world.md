This is the beginning of something I've been meaning to do for a while: write about the things I think about while building software.

## Why write?

Most of what I've learned over the past decade hasn't come from documentation — it's come from solving problems in systems that don't behave the way you'd expect. I want to start capturing some of that here.

## What to expect

I'll write about backend systems, workflow design, developer tools, and the occasional opinion on how we build things. Nothing on a schedule — just when something feels worth sharing.

If any of that sounds interesting, stick around.

## Why this matters to me

A lot of software writing online is either beginner tutorials or polished success stories. Both can be useful, but most real work lives in the middle: migrations that take longer than planned, integrations that almost work, and product decisions that trade ideal architecture for practical momentum.

That's where I've spent most of my career, and that's what I want to document.

I want these posts to be useful for people building things under real constraints — limited time, legacy systems, and teams that still need to ship.

## How I'll approach posts

When I can, I'll include concrete examples, tradeoffs, and what I would do differently in hindsight. If I share opinions, I'll explain the context behind them so you can decide whether they apply to your situation.

I'm not aiming for volume. I'm aiming for clarity.

If a post helps you avoid one expensive mistake or gives you language for a decision you're already wrestling with, that's a good outcome.
