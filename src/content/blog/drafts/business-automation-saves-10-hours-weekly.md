## Automation #1: Customer Inquiry Routing and Follow-up

### The Manual Problem
**Current Process:**
- Inquiries come via phone, email, website, social media
- Staff manually sort and respond to each inquiry
- Follow-up reminders tracked on sticky notes or spreadsheets
- Important leads fall through the cracks

**Time Cost:** 8-12 hours/week for typical small business

### The Automated Solution

**Inquiry Capture:**
- Unified contact form across all channels
- Automatic lead scoring and categorization
- Instant notification to appropriate team member

**Follow-up Sequences:**
- Automatic confirmation email to prospect
- Scheduled follow-up reminders for staff
- Automated nurture sequences for different lead types

## Getting Started: Automation Assessment

### Step 1: Track Current Time Usage
- Log time spent on repetitive tasks for one week
- Identify tasks done multiple times daily
- Note frustrations and bottlenecks

### Step 2: Prioritize by Impact
- Calculate hourly cost of manual processes
- Identify high-frequency, high-cost activities
- Consider customer impact and satisfaction

### Step 3: Start Small and Scale
- Begin with one automation
- Measure results before expanding
- Build on early wins

## Common Implementation Mistakes to Avoid

### Over-Automation
- Don't automate everything at once
- Maintain human touch where it matters
- Consider customer preferences

### Under-Planning
- Map current processes thoroughly
- Include all stakeholders in planning
- Plan for training and adoption

### Choosing Wrong Tools
- Start with business needs, not technology
- Consider integration capabilities
- Plan for growth and changes
